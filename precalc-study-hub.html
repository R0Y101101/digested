Step-by-Step Walkthroughs

Quick Reference: Section 10.6 - Parametric Equations

Plane Curve Definition

If f and g are continuous functions of t on an interval I, then the set of ordered pairs (f(t), g(t)) is a plane curve C. The equations x = f(t) and y = g(t) are parametric equations for C, and t is the parameter.

Eliminating the Parameter

Solve for t in one equation, then substitute into the other to get a rectangular equation in x and y. For trig parametric equations, use identities like sin^2 + cos^2 = 1.

Projectile Motion
x = (v0 cos a)t
y = h + (v0 sin a)t - 16t^2
Cycloid
x = a(t - sin t)
y = a(1 - cos t)
Ellipse
x = h + a cos t
y = k + b sin t
Circle
x = h + r cos t
y = k + r sin t

Rectangular to Polar

Multiple Representations

(r, θ) = (r, θ +/- 2nπ) = (-r, θ +/- (2n+1)π) for any integer n.
