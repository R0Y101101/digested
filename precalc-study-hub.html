Quick Reference: Section 10.6 - Parametric Equations

Plane Curve Definition

If f and g are continuous functions of t on an interval I, then the set of ordered pairs (f(t), g(t)) is a plane curve C. The equations x = f(t) and y = g(t) are parametric equations for C, and t is the parameter.

Eliminating the Parameter

Solve for t in one equation, then substitute into the other to get a rectangular equation in x and y. For trig parametric equations, use identities like sin²θ + cos²θ = 1.

Projectile Motion
x = (v₀ cos θ)t
y = h + (v₀ sin θ)t − 16t²
Cycloid
x = a(θ − sin θ)
y = a(1 − cos θ)
Ellipse
x = h + a cos θ
y = k + b sin θ
Circle
x = h + r cos θ
y = k + r sin θ

Quick Reference: Section 10.7 - Polar Coordinates

Coordinate Conversion

Polar → Rectangular
x = r cos θ
y = r sin θ
Rectangular → Polar
r² = x² + y²
tan θ = y/x

Multiple Representations

(r, θ) = (r, θ ± 2nπ) = (−r, θ ± (2n+1)π) for any integer n. The pole is (0, θ) for any θ.

Symmetry Tests

Quick tests: r = f(sin θ) is symmetric about θ = π/2. r = g(cos θ) is symmetric about the polar axis.
